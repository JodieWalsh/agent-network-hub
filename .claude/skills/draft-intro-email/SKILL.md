# Draft Intro Email — Interview Funnel Step 1

This skill drafts the **personal intro email** that opens our relationship with a treasured buyers agent — step 1 of the Interview Funnel (see `docs/GENEVA_ROADMAP.md` → Interview Funnel). One agent at a time, researched by hand, written like a human, sent by Jodie from her own inbox. The email introduces Buyers Agent Hub, mentions our new YouTube channel that helps buyers agents, and says **we'll call them in the next few days to invite them to be interviewed**. The call is the ask; the email opens the door warmly.

These agents are worth $1,000+/yr each and — more importantly — they're the community. Every draft should read like it could only have been written to this one person.

## What the user gives you

Research notes for ONE agent, in any messy form. Map them to these five (all optional):

1. **Standout achievement or award** — e.g. "REB Buyers Agent of the Year finalist 2025"
2. **Their patch & specialty** — e.g. "prestige coastal, Northern Beaches", "first-home buyers in Austin"
3. **Something they've written or said** — a blog post, podcast quote, LinkedIn piece
4. **A genuine specific compliment** — what actually impressed the user about them
5. **A small personal detail** — e.g. "surf lifesaver", "renovating a mid-century place"

Plus basics: their **first name** (required) and anything else offered (agency name, city).

**Graceful degradation is non-negotiable:** weave in *only* what was provided, so the email reads complete and natural at any level of detail. **NEVER emit placeholder slots** — no "[INSERT AWARD]", no "[their specialty]", ever. One genuine detail woven well beats five crammed in. If the user provides **zero** research details, don't produce a generic email — ask for at least one genuine, specific thing about the agent first. Generic is the one thing this email must never be.

## The voice (from `docs/BRAND_KIT.md` — quiet luxury, in prose)

Warm, premium, genuine, **quietly confident**. We sound like a knowledgeable colleague at a good dinner — never a megaphone, never a marketer.

- **Human, not templated.** Contractions are good. Vary sentence length. It should sound like Jodie typed it — because after polishing, she did.
- **No hype, no sales-speak.** Banned energy: "game-changing", "revolutionary", "don't miss out", "exciting opportunity", exclamation-mark pile-ups (one, at most, and only if it truly earns its place), fake urgency of any kind.
- **No email clichés.** Never open with "I hope this email finds you well" or "My name is…". Open with *them*.
- **The compliment must be specific and earned.** "Your breakdown of off-market negotiation on the Elevate podcast stuck with me" — not "I love your work!". One real compliment; flattery in bulk reads as a script.
- **Honest, always.** No invented stats, no "hundreds of agents are already…", no implying the channel is bigger or older than it is. New and genuine beats inflated. (Brand rule *and* consumer law.)
- **Globally-minded.** Plain warm English that reads the same in Sydney, Austin, or London. No country-specific idiom or assumptions unless the agent's own details supply them.
- **Modest about us.** One or two sentences on who we are, tops. We're new, we're building something considered, we admire their work — that's the whole pitch. The interview is us asking to showcase *them*, not them doing us a favour (though it is also that — hold the balance lightly).

## Structure (target 120–180 words — busy people read short emails)

1. **Subject line** — quiet, personal, specific; like a colleague's email, not a campaign. Good shapes: "Your work in [patch]", "A conversation about [specialty] — from one property person to another", "Loved your piece on [topic]". Never: "Exciting opportunity!", "Invitation to be featured ✨".
2. **Warm personal opener — the homework.** Lead with the most genuine detail from the research (the thing they'd be proudest of, or the compliment). This paragraph proves a human wrote this to them.
3. **Who we are — brief.** Jodie, co-founder of Buyers Agent Hub — a new platform and community being built for buyers agents and the professionals around them. Modest, one breath.
4. **The invitation.** We're launching a YouTube channel that helps buyers agents, and we'd love to interview them — their story, their patch, their way of working — as one of the featured voices. Frame as showcasing their expertise.
5. **The call.** "I'll give you a call in the next few days" — soft, specific, no calendar links, no "book a slot". The relationship is personal; the logistics happen on the phone.
6. **Warm sign-off.** "Warm regards," / "Looking forward to it," + **Jodie** + "Co-founder, Buyers Agent Hub". A small callback to their personal detail just before the sign-off, if one was given, lands beautifully.

## Compliance guardrails (Australian Spam Act — never soften these)

- This email is sent **one at a time, personally, from Jodie's own email account**. It is not a campaign. **Never** suggest bulk sending, mail-merge, BCC lists, sequences, or automation of any kind — if asked, explain that the one-at-a-time rule is both the legal posture and the entire point of the funnel.
- The recipient is an **`interview_outreach`** contact in Geneva: they have **not** opted in. They must never be added to Mailchimp or any mailing list off the back of this email alone.
- **If they reply yes to hearing more / staying in touch:** their consent must be **recorded in Geneva first** — edit the contact → Email Consent → Subscribed → fill "How was consent obtained?" (e.g. *"Replied yes to intro email, 12 Jul"*). The Mailchimp push is walled off until that record exists (Interview Funnel piece 2), and that's by design.
- After Jodie sends the email, remind her to move the contact's interview stage to **02 · Intro Email Sent** on their Geneva record.

## Output format

1. **Subject:** line
2. The email body in a single clean copyable block (no markdown inside it — it's going into an email client)
3. A short **"After you send"** footer: move stage to Intro Email Sent · if they reply yes to more, record consent in Geneva before any Mailchimp push · sent personally, one at a time.

Then invite edits — the user polishes and sends it herself. Offer to re-run with tweaks ("warmer", "shorter", "lead with the award instead").

## Worked example (the bar to clear)

*Research given:* Sarah Chen, buyers agent, Northern Beaches Sydney, prestige coastal specialist. REB Buyers Agent of the Year finalist 2025. Wrote a LinkedIn piece on why auction quartiles mislead buyers. Compliment: her calm, data-first manner in a market that runs on adrenaline. Personal: weekend surf lifesaver at Freshwater.

**Subject:** Your piece on auction quartiles — and a conversation

> Hi Sarah,
>
> Your LinkedIn piece on why auction quartiles mislead buyers has been sitting with me all week — it's rare to see someone in this industry argue with data instead of adrenaline, and it shows in how you work the Northern Beaches. The REB finalist nod clearly wasn't an accident.
>
> I'm Jodie, co-founder of Buyers Agent Hub — a new platform and community we're building for buyers agents and the professionals around them.
>
> We're launching a YouTube channel that helps buyers agents sharpen their craft, and I'd love to feature you — your story, your patch, the way you think about prestige coastal. A relaxed recorded conversation, nothing scripted.
>
> I'll give you a call in the next few days to see if it appeals. Enjoy the surf this weekend — Freshwater looks like it's turning it on.
>
> Warm regards,
> Jodie
> Co-founder, Buyers Agent Hub

Notice: every research detail is woven in naturally, nothing is crammed; the ask is soft; the personal detail lands last; 150 words; zero hype. If only the patch had been provided, the email would simply be shorter and lean on that — never padded, never placeholder-ed.
